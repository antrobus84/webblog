1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT has become a very important aspect of business in modern society as companies now rely on IT for communication with customers, efficiency of their operations, security of their information such as banking, documents and customer information and researching new opportunities. Using IT companies can create websites to easily connect to their customers on many different levels, whether its to collect information for marketing purposes or if its to connect with them through contact and support or even sell their products in the most efficient and fastest way possible, IT also helps with connecting the many different sites of a single business together whether that’s to communicate with other managers to find out shipment information or to coordinate company matters.

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

Firstly if you want to create a successful digital project you are going to need the right team, for that you will need a leader, someone who’s is smart and great at organising and monitoring people properly in order to make sure work is done efficiently, on time and properly, as well as plan the project appropriately to fit in the roles for each team member and the aim for the project. You will need people with skills within the area they will be working in such as IT knowledge, skills with coordinating with others and if these skills are required for the project. You will also need to plan out the risks of your project, its aim and who it is projected to, to efficiently do this it is best to create a kanban or use programs like troll to easily access this online as a team, using a kanban you can create a very organised set of objectives divided into groups for how important they are and the deadline for when they need to be done as well as select specific people to do each job.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

One of the main reasons projects fail is poor communication in a team and a lack of a leader, or a leader who lacks experience of any form of confidence to speak out when their team is doing something wrong. Poor teamwork can cause projects to slow down or just not happen at all, without communication your team will not be able to organise projects properly and may all be doing the exact same thing at once or even nothing at all and cause frustration and arguments to form. Poor planning can also ruin a project as without a proper plan and scope for the project in mind you wouldn’t know where to start and will have to plan while you create your project causing you to become less efficient and gradually slow down and fail to track progress.

https://www.projectsmart.co.uk/15-causes-of-project-failure.php

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

There are many different legal restraints which include technology and security audits, inventory management, an annual health check, business technology alignment, financial governance and information governance. GDRP is a regulation that stands for General Data Protection Regulation, they impact businesses and projects by allowing the right to access, the right to be forgotten, the right to data portability, the right to be informed, the right to have information corrected, the right to restrict processing, the right to object and the right to be notified. These will all effect projects in many ways but are there to protect a persons personal information and data. The ways these cause an impact is that they allow you to request access to your personal data and how it is being used, to transfer data from one service provider to another, to stop your data from being processed for direct marketing and to be notified when there is a breach that can compromise and individuals personal data.
